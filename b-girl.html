B-Girl

Ensure Education for every poor children

Only a small percentage of girls attending school today around the world pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM). It's even smaller in Africa, where a girl is asked to choose between STEM and her family. BLPARW is advocating to change the stereotypes and encourage girls to study STEM. We show the girls examples of successful women and mothers in STEM. We also show them how STEM impacts the world and how exciting studying STEM can be.

Our Focus

Motivate and Spark Interest

We work with schools and communities across Africa to raise awareness about global climate change concerns and why it is important to care about the environment. The programme get schoolgirls to perform simple science experiments to learn how weather affects our climate.

Through the results of these experiments, the students will reflect on the possible impacts of climate change and come up with creative responses such as artworks and short presentations to be showcased at awareness-raising events.

Inspire, Connect and Unlock

Human beings typically connect with successful people and use them as role models to inspire us to follow our dreams. It is for this reason that BLPARW connects young girls across Africa with female role models in STEM.

Our B-Girl role model programme encourages African girls to pursue STEM careers. The programme focuses on instilling confidence in the girls by hearing and learning from successful women in STEM fields who have overcome stereotypes and biases about who should be working in STEM fields.
