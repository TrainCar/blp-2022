Sauki Health

We believe good health and access to primary health care are essential for rural African communities. We provide a free service that connects people in these rural communities with primary care clinics in their locaties to encourage participation.

We work with our partners to make community medical practitioners such as doctors, nurses, and pharmacists accessible as health points of contact to rural people in Africa.

Sauki Health

Maternal Health Care

Women and newborns in remote areas in Africa often do not receive adequate maternal health care. All women need access to care and support in pregnancy, during childbirth, and in the weeks after childbirth.

The initiative ensures that rural African women seek and receive maternal health care. It raises awareness to remove barriers such as ignorance, misinformation, and cultural practices that limit access to quality maternal health services.

Sauki Health

School Based Mobile Clinic

The goal is to take adequate healthcare services to African rural communities using their local schools as a channel. It visits schools periodically offering a proactive approach to health care and helping identify silent conditions earlier.

The initiative is available to the students, each with parental consent, and also available to the schools’ staff. Intrinsically, the initiative encourages parents in rural communities to send their children to school.
