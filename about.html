About Us

Our Journey

Embracing the Past, Envisioning the Future

Established in 1987, the Better Life Program for the African Rural Woman (BLPARW) has been a beacon for change, empowering African women through health, economic, and educational programs. We initiated critical immunisation initiatives in rural Nigeria, significantly bolstering public health within communities.

Subsequently, we fostered economic independence by encouraging women's participation in cottage industries and agriculture. Additionally, we championed girls education in STEM fields, cultivating a new generation of female leaders. Over the years, our impact has been profound. With over 250 projects, we've impacted the lives of around 2.4 million individuals across three African countries, transforming communities and enhancing the quality of life.

Looking forward, we remain committed to these transformative efforts, with plans to emphasise children's education further. We believe in education as a universal right and warmly invite everyone to join our mission, envisioning an equitable future rich with opportunities where every African woman can fully realise her potential.

Our Founder

Honouring the Lifelong Impact of Mrs. Maryam Babangida

With an indomitable spirit and a relentless passion for social justice, Mrs. Babangida pioneered the transformational Better Life Program for the African Rural Woman (BLPARW). This ground-breaking initiative championed the cause of Africa's often marginalised and impoverished rural women, a testament to Mrs. Babangida's unwavering determination and commitment.

Always on the front lines, she also founded key institutions such as the National Commission for Women in Nigeria and the Maryam Babangida National Centre for Women Development in Abuja. Though she has passed on, Mrs. Babangida's legacy continues to shine as an enduring beacon of hope, inspiring future generations and propelling the quest for women's empowerment.

Chairperson

A Visionary Leader Uplifting African Women

Under the visionary leadership of our Chairperson, Aisha Babangida, BLPARW thrives as a beacon for Nigeria's underserved communities. Her commitment to empowering rural African women extends beyond national borders, resonating across the continent.

In 2016, Aisha established Egwafin Microfinance Bank, a significant leap forward in empowering local businesses with necessary financial resources. Her relentless dedication to improving rural women's living standards has earned her distinguished accolades, including the esteemed Gold Medal Award from the Crans Montana Forum, Brussels.

As a pioneering force in women's empowerment and rural development, Aisha exemplifies transformative change, ceaselessly crafting a brighter future for rural Africa.
