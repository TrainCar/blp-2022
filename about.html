About Us

Better Life Program for the African Rural Woman (BLPARW)

We are a non-profit organisation founded in 1987. The initial focus was on healthcare. We created awareness of immunization, oral rehydration therapy, and polio vaccination across rural parts of Nigeria. We later broadened our intervention to include economic empowerment. We have been supporting women in cottage industries, agriculture, and cooperative farmers. The organisation’s relaunch in 2014 has helped us extend our scope further. We now run programs such as capacity building for young women and supporting girls’ education, especially to explore STEM careers.

Founder

Mrs. Maryam Babangida

Mrs. Babangida launched the Better Life Program for the African Rural Woman in 1987 to improve the lives of rural women across Africa. These rural women are mostly side-lined, poor, and therefore the most vulnerable. The founder’s foresight, drive, determination, and commitment to excellence were responsible for the immense success of the program.

She went further to establish the National Commission for Women in Nigeria and the Maryam Babangida National Centre for Women Development in Abuja, Nigeria.

Mrs. Maryam Babangida left indelible footprints on time sands and a legacy of inestimable value.

Meet the chairperson

Chairperson

Aisha Babangida

Aisha Babangida is responsible for the overall direction of the organisation. She is a visionary leader with a history of working within philanthropy and helping underserved communities throughout Nigeria. She is devoted to the cause of the African woman through empowerment, thus fuelling her passion for doing more for the people of her country, Nigeria, and Africa.

Through Aisha’s outreach, she realised the importance of funding to small business owners. In 2016, she founded Egwafin Microfinance Bank. Egwafin helps cooperatives and small business owners gain access to finance. She has been recognised with awards at home and abroad for her work and efforts to improve the living standards of rural women.
