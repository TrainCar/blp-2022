Shea Nut Project

Shea nut is an essential source of income for several women in rural areas of West and Central Africa. But these women, who are critical players in the shea butter value chain, face tedious and laborious tasks harvesting and processing shea nuts.

Improving processing methods for rural African women will increase productivity, improve well-being and generate more income. Our Shea Nut Project will help 9,000 women process shea nuts more effectively and efficiently.

Multi Crop Project

Rural African women need help to enhance the value of their raw agricultural products through access to storage, processing facilities, and transportation.

As part of our mission to uplift rural African women through economic empowerment, we are engaging processors and storage facility owners to help 100,000 women in various capacities to increase their involvement in the agricultural value chain and integrate them into regional and global agricultural value chains.

Multi Trade Project

The project supports over 5,000 traders to establish and join cooperatives. The goal is for them to have greater control over the supply chain, help them cut costs on key inputs through economies of scale, and allow process improvements that can boost output and productivity.

Members also benefit from our entrepreneurial training, designed to help them gain financial independence with better access to the market.
